Preamble

Effective Date: March 2026

This Agreement is entered into between OPTILINE Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Agency") and Marketer Name/Company (hereinafter referred to as "the Marketer"), for the purpose of marketing the Done-For-You Digital Asset services provided by the Agency.

The parties acknowledge that this relationship is established to create a mutually beneficial partnership that drives sales growth while maintaining professional standards and legal compliance.

1Scope of Marketed Services

The Marketer is authorized to promote the Agency's core services, which include:

  • Brand Kit Identity: Comprehensive visual identity packages
  • Monthly Content Packs: Curated monthly content for social media
  • Ad Launchpad: Engineered advertising campaigns for immediate ROI

These services are offered under three distinct package tiers as detailed in Article 2.

2Commission Structure

Given that the Agency's business model is based on one-time payments with flat, transparent pricing rather than long-term contracts, the Marketer is entitled to a commission for each successfully sold service.

Commission Rates

Successful Sale Definition

A successful sale is attributed to the Marketer upon:

  1. The client's completion of payment through the Agency's designated payment gateway
  2. Submission of the Agency's Smart Onboarding Form via the Marketer's unique tracking link
  3. Confirmation of the transaction in the Agency's Verified Sales ledger

3Payment and Eligibility Terms

Commission Accrual and Payment

Commissions are due to the Marketer once the Agency has received the full payment from the client and the "Creation" phase of the service has officially commenced.

The Marketer's commission is not affected by the client's revision rounds (typically 2 to 4 rounds), as the sale is considered closed upon initial Full Delivery.

Minimum Disbursement Threshold

$100 Minimum: To optimize accounting efficiency, the minimum disbursement threshold is $100. This ensures that even a single CORE package conversion triggers a payout, maintaining high cash-flow velocity for our partners.

Payment Method

  • Traditional Payments (Credit/Debit/PayPal): Settled via PayPal
  • Cryptocurrency Payments: Settled via USDT

The Agency covers all standard PayPal-to-PayPal transaction fees, ensuring the Marketer receives their full net commission without deductions.

4Marketing Standards and Compliance

The Marketer agrees to promote the Agency in accordance with the standards outlined on the official website, specifically:

Mandatory Representations

  • No Account Management Claims: The Agency's services are not ongoing account management. Marketers must emphasize that the Agency provides production-ready assets for clients to publish.
  • 100% Client Ownership: Clearly communicate that clients retain 100% ownership of all delivered assets and files.
  • Official Delivery Timelines: Adhere to the Agency's official delivery timeframe of 7 to 21 business days based on package complexity.

Prohibited Activities

Partners must adhere to ethical marketing standards. Prohibited activities include:

  • Spamming or unsolicited outreach
  • Unauthorized automated outreach
  • Deceptive claims or misleading statements
  • False testimonials or fabricated results

Non-compliance results in immediate revocation of partner status and forfeiture of pending balances.

5Confidentiality and Intellectual Property (NDA & IP)

The Marketer agrees:

  1. Not to copy or reuse the Agency's proprietary frameworks (e.g., the Hook-Story-Offer system) to establish a competing business.
  2. All client data acquired through the Marketer's efforts remains the sole property of OPTILINE Agency. The Marketer shall not use, share, or disclose any client information without written consent from the Agency.
  3. Confidential Information includes but is not limited to: pricing models, marketing strategies, client lists, performance data, and proprietary processes.

6Commission Disbursement & Integrity

OPTILINE is committed to partner profitability. The Agency covers all standard PayPal-to-PayPal transaction fees, ensuring the Marketer receives their full net commission without deductions.

Payout Schedule

Commissions are processed and disbursed on a bi-weekly basis, provided the minimum threshold of $100 has been reached.

7Transactional Gateway Synergy

To maintain instantaneous liquidity and security, payouts are strictly matched to the client's source of funds:

  • Traditional payments (Credit/Debit/PayPal) are settled via PayPal
  • Cryptocurrency payments are settled via USDT

This ensures transparent and secure fund transfers aligned with the original transaction method.

8Currency Conversion & Mitigation

Cross-platform payout requests (e.g., requesting USDT for a PayPal-funded sale) are treated as manual exchanges and incur:

  • 10% conversion and administrative fee
  • 24-48 hour security verification window

The Marketer must submit such requests through the Agency's designated portal with proper documentation.

9Attribution & Persistent Cookies

Tracking Mechanism

Our ecosystem utilizes high-precision attribution tracking with a 60-day persistent cookie. If a referred lead converts within 60 days of their initial visit, the commission is automatically and permanently attributed to your Partner ID.

Transaction ID (Tx ID)

Every sale is logged with a unique Transaction ID in our Verified Sales ledger for complete transparency and audit purposes.

10Compliance & Brand Representation

Partners must adhere to ethical marketing standards and represent the Agency professionally at all times. The Agency reserves the right to:

  • Monitor marketing activities for compliance
  • Request modifications to marketing materials
  • Suspend or terminate partnership for violations

Non-compliance results in immediate revocation of partner status and forfeiture of pending balances.

11Internal Transaction Mitigation

To prevent system exploitation, "Self-Referrals" (purchasing services for personal use via one's own partner link) are strictly prohibited.

The Agency's AI-driven audit system monitors for IP and identity overlaps to ensure ecosystem fairness. Violations will result in:

  • Forfeiture of commissions on flagged transactions
  • Potential termination of the partnership agreement

12Refunds, Chargebacks & Clawback Clause

Refund Policy

In the event that a client requests a refund or initiates a chargeback after the Agency has paid the Marketer's commission, the following applies:

Clawback Mechanism:

  • The Agency shall deduct the full commission amount from the Marketer's future earnings (Clawback)
  • If the Marketer has no pending balance, the amount is recorded as a debit against future commissions
  • The Marketer remains liable for any negative balance until settled

Dispute Resolution

The Marketer may dispute a clawback within 7 days of notification by providing evidence of service delivery or client satisfaction. The Agency will review and respond within 5 business days.

13Independent Contractor Clause

Relationship Definition

The Marketer and the Agency acknowledge and agree that:

  1. The Marketer is an Independent Contractor – not an employee, agent, or representative of the Agency
  2. No Employment Relationship – This agreement does not create an employment relationship, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship
  3. No Legal Representation – The Marketer is not authorized to represent, bind, or make commitments on behalf of OPTILINE Agency
  4. Tax Responsibility – The Marketer is solely responsible for:
    • All applicable taxes (federal, state, local, and international)
    • Self-employment taxes
    • Business expenses and insurance
    • Maintaining proper business records

No Benefits

The Marketer is not entitled to any employee benefits, including health insurance, retirement plans, paid leave, or workers' compensation.

14Brand License & Usage

Limited License Grant

The Agency grants the Marketer a limited, non-exclusive, and revocable license to use:

  • OPTILINE logos and branding materials
  • Marketing collateral and promotional assets
  • Proprietary frameworks and methodologies

Permitted Use: Promotion of the Agency's services as outlined in this agreement

Prohibited Use:

  • Creating derivative works or modified versions of Agency materials
  • Using Agency branding for competing services
  • Sublicensing or transferring rights to third parties
  • Using materials after termination of this agreement

Asset Ownership

All marketing materials, logos, and brand assets remain the exclusive property of OPTILINE Agency. The Marketer receives only a usage license, not ownership.

Immediate Cessation Upon Termination

The Marketer agrees to immediately cease all use of Agency assets and branding upon termination of this agreement. Failure to comply may result in legal action.

15Termination Clause

Termination Rights

Either party may terminate this agreement at any time and for any reason by providing written notice (email is acceptable) with a minimum of 7 days advance notice.

Termination Procedures

  1. Notice Requirement: Termination notice must be sent to the designated contact email address of the other party
  2. Effective Date: Termination becomes effective 7 days after notice is received
  3. Final Commission: All commissions earned and accrued prior to the termination date remain due and payable

Post-Termination Obligations

Upon termination:

  • The Marketer must immediately cease all promotional activities using Agency materials
  • All Agency branding and assets must be removed from the Marketer's platforms
  • The Marketer remains bound by confidentiality and IP protection clauses
  • Pending commissions will be paid according to the regular disbursement schedule

Survival Clauses

The following articles survive termination indefinitely:

  • Article 5: Confidentiality and Intellectual Property
  • Article 13: Independent Contractor Clause
  • Article 14: Brand License & Usage
  • Article 16: Limitation of Liability
  • Article 17: Dispute Resolution

16Limitation of Liability

Liability Caps

Neither party shall be liable to the other for:

  • Indirect, incidental, or consequential damages
  • Lost profits or revenue
  • Loss of business opportunity
  • Reputational harm

The Agency's total liability under this agreement shall not exceed the total commissions paid to the Marketer in the 12 months preceding the claim.

17Dispute Resolution

Informal Resolution

In the event of a dispute, the parties agree to attempt resolution through good-faith negotiation within 14 days of written notice.

Escalation

If informal resolution fails, disputes shall be resolved through:

  1. Mediation (if both parties agree)
  2. Binding Arbitration under applicable law
  3. Legal Action as a last resort
